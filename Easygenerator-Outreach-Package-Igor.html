Funnel Audit — AAA Diagnostic

The Demand Engine as It Stands

Attraction → Alignment → Activation. Three layers scored. The biggest revenue leak identified.

Attraction — 6/10

Gap Present

Strong SEO comparison pages (Articulate, iSpring, Adobe Captivate, Elucidat alternatives) capturing high-intent evaluation queries. €2M+ in paid. But: GEO presence is 4/10. Attribution is broken — paid spend optimised for clicks, not pipeline. Intent-based content cluster architecture does not yet exist.

Alignment — 5/10

High Gap

Free trial model is correct and low friction. But enterprise buyers (Siemens, Kellogg's calibre) see a pricing page starting at $116/month — a signal that reads as SMB self-serve, not enterprise-grade. No vertical landing pages. No segment-aware messaging. Enterprise CTA ("contact us") likely buried below the fold. One-size-fits-all funnel for a three-segment market.

Activation — 4/10

High Gap

14-day trial with full access and no credit card is structurally correct. But no evidence of a structured activation email sequence driving users to "first published course" within 72 hours. No defined onboarding milestone tracking. Enterprise demo to contract journey is undefined — no internal champion enablement kit, no ROI calculator. Trial users who don't self-activate are silently lost.

Biggest Leak

The trial activation sequence does not exist. The moment a user signs up for the 14-day trial is the highest-leverage moment in the funnel. If they don't publish a first course within 72 hours, conversion probability drops by more than half. This is not a traffic problem. This is a post-signup infrastructure gap — and it costs more revenue than the GEO gap and the attribution gap combined.
