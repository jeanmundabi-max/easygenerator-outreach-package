Common Questions Before the Pilot

The concerns every Learning and Development leader raises before they start

These are the exact questions enterprise Learning and Development teams ask before running the pilot. Answered directly.

Our subject matter experts are not instructional designers. Will the quality be good enough?

The authoring tool is built specifically for people with no instructional design background. It guides experts through learning objectives, question types, and course structure step by step. The artificial intelligence writing assistant helps them structure their knowledge into a learning sequence without needing to know how to design a course. The Learning and Development team reviews and approves before anything goes live. Enterprise teams consistently find that courses built by domain experts are more accurate, more current, and more practically useful than courses built by generalist agencies.

How do we maintain quality and compliance if non-specialists are creating training?

Quality control stays with the Learning and Development team. Easygenerator has a configurable review and approval workflow. No course goes live without your team’s sign-off. You set the governance rules: which course types need one reviewer versus two, which departments can self-publish, and which require compliance sign-off. The Learning and Development team’s role shifts from production to quality oversight. They review 20 courses a week instead of building 2.

Does Easygenerator integrate with our existing learning management system?

Yes. Easygenerator supports SCORM 1.2, SCORM 2004, xAPI, and LTI — the four major integration standards. Published courses can be exported directly into your existing learning management system. Completion data, quiz scores, and time-on-course data flow through the integration in real time. The enterprise pilot includes a technical session to confirm compatibility before go-live.

We operate across 30 countries. Can Easygenerator support multiple languages and regional compliance?

Decentralised authoring is precisely what makes multi-market training scalable. Regional subject matter experts create training in their own language, for their own compliance context, at their own pace. Central Learning and Development sets the governance framework and approves. The platform interface supports 30 plus languages. This is the model Danone used to extend training coverage to every market without adding Learning and Development headcount in any region.

We already have an authoring tool. Why would we switch?

Easygenerator is not a replacement for a professional authoring tool for your Learning and Development team. It adds a creation layer designed for subject matter experts rather than instructional designers. Most enterprise clients run both: a traditional authoring tool for complex, high-production-value courses built by Learning and Development specialists, and Easygenerator for the high-volume expert-led training that makes up 80 percent of an organisation’s actual training needs. The pilot is a practical test of whether that hybrid model fits your organisation.
